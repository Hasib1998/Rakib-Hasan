Research

The Honors Program in Economics gives outstanding senior majors an opportunity to design and carry out a research project on a topic of their choice, under close supervision of faculty. Students who are interested in pursuing honors in economics should apply during their junior year. The research project culminates in a senior thesis which, together with the student's record in economics courses, forms the basis for faculty decisions on the award of departmental honors. The program is administered by the Department's Committee on Undergraduate Honors and Awards. The Committee consists of the Director of Undergraduate Studies and two faculty appointed by the Chair of the Department. (The faculty member directing the Senior Seminar, Economics 398, is not eligible to serve on the Committee.) The Director of Undergraduate Studies serves as the departmental honors coordinator.
